Case Studies

Deep dives into projects that combine planning, surveying, and GIS.

Case Study 1: Estate Masterplan & Subdivision

Type: Physical Planning • Role: Lead Planner & GIS Support

Context

A large estate required regularization of land use, subdivision of plots, and infrastructure planning to align with county policies and long-term growth.

Objectives

  • Develop a land use structure with clear residential, commercial, institutional, and open spaces.
  • Design a logical road network and pedestrian circulation.
  • Integrate drainage, riparian buffers, and environmental constraints.

Approach

  • Base mapping from survey data, RIMs, and aerial imagery.
  • GIS analysis for slopes, hydrology, and access.
  • Iterative planning from concepts to final subdivision layout.

Outputs

  • Estate masterplan layout.
  • Subdivision plan with plot sizes and road hierarchy.
  • Formal planning brief for county approvals.

Case Study 2: Topo-Cadastral Survey for Institutional Land

Type: Survey & GIS • Role: Survey Assistant / GIS Analyst

Context

An institution needed accurate topo-cadastral data to support internal planning, new buildings, and infrastructure upgrades.

Objectives

  • Capture boundaries, features, and elevation data.
  • Provide base maps for architects and engineers.
  • Support regularisation of land records.

Approach

  • GNSS and Total Station observations with control checks.
  • Generation of contours and feature mapping.
  • Production of CAD and GIS-ready layers.

Outputs

  • Topo-cadastral plans in CAD and PDF.
  • Digital GIS layers for planning and asset management.

Case Study 3: Riparian Mapping & Policy Support

Type: GIS & Policy • Role: Lead GIS Analyst / Co-Author

Context

Rapid urbanization along rivers created tension between development and environmental protection. This project mapped riparian zones to inform planning and enforcement.

Objectives

  • Map river channels and riparian buffers using GIS.
  • Identify encroached or high-risk areas.
  • Support planning recommendations and policy discussions.

Approach

  • Use of DEMs, satellite imagery, and existing datasets.
  • Hydrological analysis and buffer creation in GIS.
  • Preparation of clear, decision-oriented map outputs.

Outputs

  • Riparian zone maps for technical and stakeholder meetings.
  • Inputs to a conference paper and planning guidelines.
